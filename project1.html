2. Project Introduction

Maatram On Trust proposes a School Kitchen Gardening Project at the selected Government School to create a hands-on environmental learning experience for students. This initiative transforms unused school spaces into productive gardens, promoting sustainability, nutrition awareness, and agricultural education. It serves as a model for eco-friendly learning benefiting students, teachers, and the community.

3. Project Objectives

  • Create a functional and sustainable kitchen garden.
  • Educate students on organic farming and healthy eating.
  • Integrate gardening with academic and life skills.
  • Promote teamwork, responsibility, environmental awareness.
  • Support school‚Äôs midday meals with fresh herbs and vegetables.

4. Target Beneficiaries

  • Primary & Secondary Students
  • School Eco-Club Members
  • Teachers & Volunteers
  • Community Members involved in sustainability

5. Project Activities

5.1 Space Inspection & Planning

  • Identify suitable spaces
  • Plan layout for beds, compost, water access

5.2 Soil Preparation & Setup

  • Clear area
  • Use grow bags/raised beds
  • Mix soil, compost, coco peat
  • Install drip system (optional)

5.3 Training & Awareness

  • Basics of kitchen gardening
  • Importance of organic food
  • Seed sowing, plant care
  • Composting food waste

5.4 Gardening Materials Distribution

  • Grow bags / Raised beds
  • Vegetable seeds (greens, tomato, chilli, etc.)
  • Saplings (tomato, brinjal, curry leaves)
  • Compost & soil mix
  • Gardening tools

5.5 Demonstration Garden

  • Daily upkeep
  • Rotational watering
  • Organic pest control

5.6 Monitoring & Support

  • Weekly volunteer visits
  • Guidance via photos/WhatsApp
  • Crop planning
  • Monthly performance reports

6. Expected Outcomes

  • Organic garden inside the school
  • Better understanding of biology & sustainability
  • Teamwork and life-skill development
  • Greener environment inside school
  • Optional meal system support
  • Strong environmental culture among students

7. Impact Indicators

  • Number of student participants
  • Variety of vegetables grown
  • Seasonal harvest quantity
  • Waste reduced through composting
  • Student learning outcomes
